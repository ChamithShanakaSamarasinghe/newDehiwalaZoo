Why choose us


One of Asia's oldest zoological gardens is Dehiwala Zoological Garden. It features a large collection of animals from all around the world. It is accessible by public transit and is open all year.   1. Shrub and vine refer to a plant's growth habit rather than a taxonomic classification. Because of their visible efforts to find support for their thin stems, vines are one of the easiest to distinguish of the two. Shrubs, on the other hand, can readily be mistaken for trees with careful pruning. Shrubs are multi-stemmed woody plants that grow shorter than trees, according to botany. Plants classified as shrubs can grow as tall as trees depending on their growing conditions. Scrub or shrubland are habitats that are largely made up of shrub-like plants and are classed according to how dense the vegetation cover is. A closed-scrub ecology, for example, has denser foliage than an open-scrub ecosystem.

Dehiwala Zoo

About the zoo

  1. The National Zoological Gardens, also known as the Dehiwala Zoo, is a 30 acre well-landscaped zoo with a diverse assortment of birds, reptiles, fish, and mammals. One of Asia's oldest zoos is the Dehiwala Zoo. John Hagenbeck, the brother of Carl Hagenbeck, a well-known animal trainer and dealer, founded the zoo as a menagerie in the early twentieth century.

Ostriches

There are many ostriches in the zoo in which some of them are locally breeded and some ar being bought from other countries.

Parrots

At the zoo's other pond, visitors can see a variety of free-living aquatic and semi-aquatic birds. Around the pond are nesting Night Herons (Nycticorax nycticorax), Cormorants (Phalacocorax species), Egrets (Egretta species), and Spotted Billed Pelicans (Pelecanus philippensis).

Lions

The Sri Lankan lion (Panthera leo sinhaleyus), sometimes known as the Ceylonese lion, is a prehistoric lion that was discovered in Sri Lanka. It is thought to have gone extinct around 37,000 years ago, before the appearance of culturally sophisticated humans. Only two teeth from this lion have been discovered in Kuruwita deposits. P. Deraniyagala proposed this subspecies in 1939 based on these teeth.

Tiger

They are mammals with hooves on their feet. They are classed as even toed mammals or odd toed mammals based on the number of hooves. Visitors are immediately captivated by a troop of Chapman's Zebras at the zoo's entrance. Visitors take advantage of the opportunity to pose for a snapshot in front of the zebra enclosure, where they can observe the Zebra family galloping, jumping, and feeding.
